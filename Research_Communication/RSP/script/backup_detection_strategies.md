# Backup — Detection strategies and event definitions

## When to use
If the panel asks "how will you detect events?", "what thresholds will you use?", or "how do other studies define drought-to-flood transitions?"

These two slides go together. The first shows the three strategies conceptually, the second shows what specific choices studies actually made.

---

### Slide: Three strategies for detecting transitions

There are three ways studies go about detecting these transitions. The most common is to threshold streamflow directly: anything below the 20th percentile is drought, anything above the 90th is flood, and then you check whether those episodes happened close enough together in time. The second way is to compute an index first, like SPI from precipitation, and threshold that instead. And the third way is to classify atmospheric circulation patterns and say the transition is when one regime shifts to another. No streamflow or precipitation involved at that level.

The first two both threshold something in the end. But Yang et al. applied both to the same US catchments over seven decades and got opposite results. Streamflow said transitions are becoming less frequent. SPI said more frequent. Same catchments, same period.

### Slide: How are drought-to-flood events defined?

[click] So this table is the actual choices five studies made. They all pick a drought threshold and a flood threshold, but they disagree on the numbers. The drought threshold goes from the 15th percentile to the 50th. The flood threshold goes from the 50th percentile of annual maxima to the 99th. And the time allowed between drought ending and flood starting goes from no gap to 90 days.

The last row is Wang et al. from China. They don't threshold streamflow. They compute an alternation index from SPI and threshold that, which is strategy 2 on the previous slide. The index has coefficients calibrated for monsoon basins, so it wouldn't transfer to Ireland without reworking.

Anderson et al. tested several of these on eight catchments where transitions were known to have happened, and only picked up three. For Ireland, none of these have been tried.
