### Slide 5 — What's Next

Where does this leave me, and what's next? Between now and August I have three concrete targets.

First, finish the system comparison. I have the deterministic results for temperature, precipitation, and wind. What remains is extending that to probabilistic metrics and writing it up. The preliminary finding is that the two systems show near-identical skill per initialisation at weekly timescales, which means the subseasonal system's advantage is its much denser temporal sampling, not better atmospheric skill. That result determines which dataset the rest of the work builds on.

Second, begin transition detection for Irish catchments. That means applying multiple event definitions to Irish river and precipitation data and seeing whether they detect known events.

Third, I have two presentations coming up. I've submitted a poster abstract to Met Éireann's Research Showcase in May, and I'm presenting a poster at EMS in September. The EMS poster covers the system comparison and the first results on multi-hazard skill, using case studies of Irish flood and drought events.
