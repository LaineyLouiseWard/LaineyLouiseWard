## PARTICIPATE workshop — articulate your research

### Articulate your research (~30 seconds)

My PhD is looking at predicting droughts and floods but from a multi-hazard perspective. So, about a quarter of all floods globally are preceded by drought. In these cases, the flooding afterwards can be worse because the ground can't absorb the rain. The problem is that sectors like agriculture and reinsurance still treat drought and flood as separate hazards, which means they are underestimating the true risk profile. On top of that, we have climate change, which means we will be experiencing these hazards more often and more severely. So for in order for Ireland to be climate resilient, we need to be forecasting the combined risk from different hazards, not just each hazard on its own. And that's the core of my research.

## Innovation / entrepreneurship angle

### What does it actually take to turn an idea into impact?

To turn an idea into impact you need to keep stakeholders and practitioners in consideration from the very start. It requires you to create something someone will actually use, not just something that works in a paper, or research in a vacuum. 

### When does research become innovation?

Research becomes innovation when it stops answering questions that only matter to researchers, and instead answers questions that matter outside of academia in the operational world. It's when it goes from being theoretical to being practical. So I guess, research becomes innovation when the framing changes.

### What role should researchers play in entrepreneurship?

SO ideally, researchers shouldnt be just passing off their research off to someone else in the entrepreneurship pipeline they should be in the room from the start. Researchers know what the science can and can't do, and so they can help and push the limits of the product or service youre trying to build.

### What makes an idea worth pursuing?

An idea is worth pursuing when there's an overlap between "nobody has done this" and "somebody needs this." If people are making big decisions based on half the information they should have, and you can see exactly what's missing and how to get that to then, then that's worth pursuing.

## Vision and big thinking

### If your research succeeds, what changes in the real world?

If my research succeeds, I could see a farmer getting early warnings 6 weeks out that weather conditions are about to shift from one hazard state to another. I could see a reinsurance company pricing the combined risk of droughts and floods properly, instead of treating them as two separate things.
Ultimately, I Ireland would be chnaged to be more climate resilient, both economically and for the communities that are affected.

### What future are you personally trying to build?

Right now we treat every hazard in isolation, and the risk profiles don't reflect how they actually interact. The future I'm trying to build is one where they do, so that Ireland's climate resilience is based on the full picture, not half of it.

### What's something people underestimate about the problem you're working on?

How much the science disagrees with itself. The same phenomenon has five or six different names, and different ways of measuring it give opposite trends over the same time period. We're still trying to agree on best practises and standardised practises moving forward. 

### What would success look like 10 years from now?

In ten years I'd like to see national meteorlogical services like Met Eireann issuing multi-hazard forecasts. I'd also like to see various sectors like agriculture and reinsurance have those forecasts personalised for their needs.
