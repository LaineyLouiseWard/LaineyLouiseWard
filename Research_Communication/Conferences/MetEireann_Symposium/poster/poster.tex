% ROW 1 LEFT: 1 — Motivation
%----------------------------------------------------------------------
\headerbox{\sectionnum{1}Motivation}{name=motivation,column=0,row=0,span=1}{

Floods and droughts can occur in sequence, with one intensifying the other.
Globally, approximately 25\% of floods are preceded by drought~[1],
and socio-economic losses from these compound events can be up to
eight times greater than from isolated hazards~[2].

\vspace{0.4em}

Forecasting these transitions at subseasonal-to-seasonal lead times
(2~weeks to 2~months) could support water resource management, agriculture,
and disaster preparedness --- but \textbf{no study has attempted it}.

\vspace{0.4em}

Predicting drought-to-flood transitions requires verified forecast skill
at every level, from meteorological variables through hydrological response
to event detection (Figure~1). \textbf{This work begins at the foundation:}
does the ECMWF extended-range system carry useful skill for basic
meteorological variables over Ireland?
}

\headerbox{\sectionnum{5}Discussion \& Future Work}{name=discussion,column=0,span=3,below=data}{

\begin{minipage}[t]{0.62\linewidth}
\textbf{Discussion}

\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=1em, nosep]
\item First assessment of extended-range forecast skill over Ireland at
      subseasonal-to-seasonal lead times
\item Forecasts carry useful skill for 2\,m temperature through Week~6,
      with CRPSS remaining positive across the full domain
\item Both the subseasonal and seasonal systems offer comparable probabilistic
      performance, suggesting either can serve as a basis for downstream
      applications
\item Probabilistic framing more robust than deterministic --- ensemble
      spread adds value beyond the ensemble mean
\end{itemize}
\end{minipage}%
\hfill
\begin{minipage}[t]{0.33\linewidth}
\textbf{Future Work} {\small (Figure~1, levels above)}

\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=1em, nosep]
\item Extend verification to precipitation
\item Trace skill through hydrological variables (soil moisture, runoff)
\item Define and verify drought-to-flood transition events from ensemble
      forecast trajectories
\item Explore ML for transition probability estimation
\end{itemize}

\end{minipage}
}
